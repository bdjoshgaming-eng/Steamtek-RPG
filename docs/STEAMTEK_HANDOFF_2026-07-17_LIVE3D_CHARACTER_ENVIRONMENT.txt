CURRENT PROJECT DIRECTION

Steamtek is a neo-industrial cyberpunk-steampunk isometric RPG. The surface world is intentionally compact—apartment, alley, street, and bar—while most gameplay occurs underground in the silo. The target presentation is a high-fidelity 2.5D hybrid: live 3D characters, effects, collision, and transitions are viewed through a locked isometric-style camera, while environment art may use hand-painted textures and carefully layered visual assets. The palette is dark steel, concrete, copper, cyan, magenta, and amber, with rain, steam, and wet surfaces. Avoid ornate Victorian steampunk styling.

IMPORTANT CORRECTION: modular_v1 is not the project standard. Do not describe it as the standard and do not rebuild current work around it. The current authority is the approved live-3D scale and camera contract. Reusable packed scenes such as rain and steam effects are ordinary reusable Godot components; they do not make the environment part of modular_v1 or impose a modular snap system.

The user is not an artist and needs a workflow that is repeatable, beginner-friendly, and resistant to manual rework. Environment art should fit the measured live-3D shell. Never resize the approved protagonist to compensate for old artwork. Old apartment imagery may remain only as a visual or technical reference when explicitly labeled as not to scale.
