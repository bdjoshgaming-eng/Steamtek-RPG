# Steamtek World Color Progression

Date locked: 2026-07-14

## Core world structure

The vivid rain-soaked city-street direction applies specifically to the
surface. Most of Steamtek takes place below it in a massive underground city
and silo. The world's color, light, maintenance, and atmosphere change as the
player descends.

Depth is a continuous visual and narrative gradient, not a switch between two
unrelated art styles. Every layer remains recognizably Steamtek and retains the
neo-industrial / neo-punk identity.

## Surface

The surface is the most openly vibrant and rain-reflective layer.

- Cyan, electric blue, magenta, pink, violet, and amber focal lighting
- Commercial signs, illuminated windows, active street furniture, and venues
- Strong colored reflections across wet roads and sidewalks
- Dark industrial materials used as a stage for visible civic life
- The Lantern as a prominent colorful social destination

## Upper underground city

The inhabited upper city still contains strong color, but it is more enclosed,
artificial, and infrastructure-dependent.

- Vibrant commercial, transit, entertainment, and community pockets
- Colored machinery, signs, cables, windows, kiosks, and navigation lighting
- Longer stretches of cool utility light between colorful destinations
- More exposed pipes, ventilation, pressure systems, repairs, and crowding
- Reflected color on damp floors and metal rather than open-rain reflections

## Middle depths

The middle city uses an uneven mixture of color and darkness.

- Saturation becomes localized into islands of habitation and functioning tech
- Lighting is less consistent and maintenance wear is more visible
- Industrial amber, warning red, cyan status light, and improvised magenta or
  blue signage carry more of the palette
- Residential and civic color survives through customization, graffiti,
  textiles, painted repairs, and scavenged lighting
- Shadows expand, but silhouettes and traversal remain readable

## Deep silo

The deeper silo becomes darker, grittier, dirtier, and more oppressive without
becoming generic monochrome industrial horror.

- Fewer dependable light sources and broader pools of darkness
- Heavier corrosion, soot, oil, water damage, patched machinery, and exposed
  structural systems
- Color appears selectively through emergency systems, luminous fluids,
  failing displays, pressure indicators, graffiti, faction markings, and
  character equipment
- Cyan, blue, magenta, pink, violet, amber, and warning red remain present but
  become scarcer, sharper, and more narratively meaningful
- Neo-punk identity survives through human modification, defiant color,
  improvised technology, layered signage, and visible subculture

## Global rules

- Descending reduces the amount, reliability, and distribution of vibrant
  color; it does not remove color from the setting.
- Darker areas must retain readable silhouettes, navigation cues, and important
  interactive details.
- Do not let deep environments drift into Victorian steampunk, fantasy dungeon,
  generic brown rust, or featureless blue-gray sci-fi corridors.
- Color can identify districts, factions, safety systems, economic conditions,
  and functioning versus failing infrastructure.
- Practical light sources must explain their nearby reflected color.
- Each depth band needs quiet areas and vivid focal points; only their ratio and
  intensity change.
