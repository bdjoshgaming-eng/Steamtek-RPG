# STEAMPUNK_STYLE_MEMORY

## Steamtek — Master Style Brief

### Core Identity

Steamtek is a gritty, hand-painted, stylized neo-industrial cyberpunk world with modern steampunk mechanics and an Arcane-inspired painterly finish.

The setting should feel industrial, mechanical, moody, lived-in, colorful, slightly cinematic, grounded in utility and structure, and visually energized by neon and emissive technology.

The world is **not Victorian**. Avoid old-timey steampunk clichés. The intended result is a modern industrial future infused with steam-tech engineering.

---

## Style Mix

Use this balance as the default:

- **40% cyberpunk**
- **20% neo-industrial**
- **20% modern steampunk**
- **20% Arcane-inspired painterly treatment**

### Cyberpunk — 40%

Cyberpunk drives the color palette, lighting accents, emissive elements, screens, interfaces, urban-futurist atmosphere, tech surfaces, and visual energy.

### Neo-industrial — 20%

Neo-industrial drives the structure, architecture, materials, heavy utility-first construction, exposed infrastructure, concrete, steel, vents, panels, and mechanical practicality.

### Modern steampunk — 20%

Modern steampunk drives copper and brass accents, pressure systems, valves, pipes, steam-tech housings, and retro-mechanical engineering flavor.

This influence must remain modern and functional, not decorative or Victorian.

### Arcane-inspired painterly treatment — 20%

The Arcane-inspired influence drives the hand-painted finish, stylized material rendering, painterly texture work, richer color harmony, expressive lighting, dramatic visual storytelling, and polished game-art presentation.

This affects rendering and presentation, not the setting’s genre.

---

## Neon and Color Rules

### Neon Presence

Neon should be:

> Clearly visible and identity-defining, but balanced.

It should sit between subtle and prominent:

- More than tiny accent lighting
- Less than a fully neon-saturated world
- Strong enough to be part of Steamtek’s identity
- Restrained enough that industrial materials remain dominant

### Preferred Neon Colors

- Cyan
- Pink
- Magenta
- Bright green

### Acceptable Neon Uses

- Signage
- Monitor screens
- UI panels
- Status lights
- Glowing tubes
- Trim lighting
- Control panels
- Tech seams
- Small machinery details
- Reflected light on nearby metal or concrete

### Avoid

- Covering every surface in glowing light
- Making assets entirely neon
- Letting emissive color overpower form or readability
- Turning scenes into clean sci-fi light spam

---

## Material Language

Base the world on grounded industrial materials such as:

- Gunmetal
- Dark steel
- Worn steel
- Concrete
- Oxidized metal
- Rusted or weathered panels
- Blackened iron
- Copper
- Brass
- Rubber
- Aged glass
- Industrial plastics
- Worn leather
- Matte painted surfaces

The world should feel built, not decorative.

Materials should communicate wear, grime, use, age, maintenance history, and practical construction.

Good surface storytelling includes:

- Chipped paint
- Grime in recesses
- Worn edges
- Soot or oil staining
- Faded labels
- Hazard striping
- Scuffed floors
- Patched metal plates
- Discolored heat-exposed machinery

---

## Shape Language and Form

Use readable, practical, game-friendly silhouettes.

### Preferred Forms

- Blocky industrial shapes
- Sturdy proportions
- Layered paneling
- Modular construction
- Exposed fasteners where useful
- Armored housings
- Reinforced corners
- Visible functional components
- Believable thickness

Steamtek assets should feel functional, engineered, slightly rugged, stylized but believable, and readable at gameplay distance.

### Avoid

- Ornate ornamentation
- Fragile decorative flourishes
- Excessive curves without functional logic
- Whimsical fantasy detailing
- Overly sleek or sterile sci-fi minimalism

---

## Architecture and Environment Direction

Steamtek environments should feel like a massive underground industrial civilization.

Common spaces include:

- Apartment interiors
- Industrial corridors
- Workshops
- Markets
- Utility zones
- Mechanical districts
- Silo infrastructure

Use concrete walls, metal wall panels, industrial floor plates, vents, pipes, exposed conduits, steam lines, pressure systems, machinery housings, neon signage, glowing panels, worn apartment furniture, and modular industrial construction.

Environments should feel moody, atmospheric, dense but readable, lived-in, practical, colorful in lighting, and grounded in structure.

---

## Furniture and Props Direction

Props and furniture should be grounded in industrial materials, cyberpunk through lighting and emissive color, modern steampunk through copper, brass, and pressure-tech details, and Arcane-inspired through the hand-painted finish and mood.

Good prop traits include:

- Armored furniture shells
- Practical metal frames
- Industrial fittings
- Small illuminated controls
- Neon indicator strips
- Copper trim or piping
- Modular utility design
- Worn upholstery
- Mechanical housings
- Visible panel seams

Props should not feel like generic modern furniture, pure sci-fi plastic, rustic fantasy, Victorian antiques, or comical steampunk gear clutter.

---

## Texture and Rendering Direction

The art style should be hand-painted and stylized, not photorealistic.

### Texture Goals

- Painterly surface treatment
- Readable material separation
- Broad value shapes
- Controlled edge highlights
- Intentional wear
- Stylized shading
- Rich but disciplined color

Textures should emphasize readability, mood, game-art clarity, material identity, and stylized craftsmanship.

### Avoid

- Noisy realism
- Excessive micro-detail
- Muddy unreadable surfaces
- Flat unshaded color fields
- Cheap plastic-looking materials unless intentional

---

## What Steamtek Is Not

Steamtek is not:

- Victorian steampunk
- Ornate gearpunk
- Fantasy tavern steampunk
- Clean sterile sci-fi
- Ultra-minimal cyberpunk
- Brown-only industrial grit
- Fully neon-soaked nightclub futurism
- Retro dieselpunk
- Whimsical steampunk cosplay

### Hard Avoid List

- Top hats
- Corsets
- Monocles
- Decorative gears used only as ornament
- Ornate Victorian filigree
- Old London architecture
- Brass overload without industrial grounding
- Overly clean white sci-fi panels
- Generic fantasy motifs

---

## Design Priorities

When in doubt, prioritize in this order:

1. Readable silhouette
2. Strong industrial construction
3. Cyberpunk color and emissive identity
4. Modern steampunk mechanical flavor
5. Painterly Arcane-inspired finish
6. Wear, grime, and lived-in storytelling
