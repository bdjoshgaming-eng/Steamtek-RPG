During this conversation, the project direction was first recalibrated against modern isometric games, especially *The Ascent*, because the original character and environment presentation felt oversized. The first surface district was redefined as a sparse, west-to-east opening route rather than the earlier north-to-south district layout. The intended game opening is: wake in the apartment, learn movement, read the quest note, open the storage box, collect and equip the crafting tool, mineral survey kit, brass knuckles, and gun, then leave through the east door. The surface tutorial continues east through an optional loot alley, a cul-de-sac combat encounter, additional thugs and the first skill/XP lesson, the garage and its initially locked crafting bench, The Lantern for quest turn-in and vendor training, and finally the maintenance lift into the first silo/Undertown district. That wider surface route remains the narrative plan, but active production was deliberately narrowed to the apartment interior so it can be treated as a polished, self-contained opening demo.

The apartment uses Shadowrun Returns' hand-painted modular-environment production logic as its presentation benchmark while retaining original Steamtek designs. The locked visual mixture is **40% neo-industrial, 40% cyberpunk, and 20% practical steampunk**. The target is a dark, practical industrial home with blackened steel and gunmetal construction, worn floor panels, exposed pressure infrastructure, embedded interfaces, segmented technical framing, controlled cyan/magenta signals, warm amber domestic lighting, and visible painted brushwork. It must not drift into Victorian ornament, decorative gears, generic glossy 3D, or indiscriminate neon. The apartment should feel lived-in but still leave enough open floor area for comfortable movement. Wear and grime may be painted into materials, but authored objects must never be baked into furniture or background textures.

A new plug-and-play apartment interior library has been installed. Architecture lives under `res://scenes/environment/live3d/kits/apartment_interior/` and currently contains nine modular scenes: 1.2 m and 2.4 m floor tiles, a service-grate overlay, 1.2 m and 2.4 m solid walls, a window wall, a door wall, a half-height partition, and a corner column. A cup, book, tool case, or other small object can therefore be moved, replaced, or deleted without modifying the table, shelf, or surrounding room.
