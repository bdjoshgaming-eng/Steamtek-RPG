COMBAT ARCHITECTURE (current)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Two autoloads own combat:

  Combat.gd      - all combat MATH and resolution   (20 funcs)
  CombatData.gd  - all combat DATA and tables       (6 funcs)

main.gd calls into them; it holds no combat formulas. (191 funcs)

MASTER DAMAGE FORMULA (Phase 3), all factors explicit:
  Damage = WeaponDamage
         x AbilityCoefficient
         x ClassModifier          (placeholder 1.0)
         x CertificationModifier
         x CriticalModifier
         x WeakPointModifier      (placeholder 1.0, deferred)

Ability coefficients: Basic 1.0 -> Novice 1.5 -> Tier II 2.5 ->
Master AoE 4.0 -> utility 0.25.  (SWG-faithful spread.)

MITIGATION CURVE (Phase 4a), used for armor AND resistances:
  DamageReduction = Rating / (Rating + 140)

DAMAGE TYPES (Phase 4b) - Steamtek-native, 7 total:
  Kinetic, Ballistic, Thermal, Pressure, Arc, Chemical, EMP
  Live on weapons: Kinetic 8, Ballistic 7, Pressure 2, Thermal 1,
  Arc 1. Chemical and EMP have NO weapons yet - columns inert.
  Melee flavor (Slashing/Blunt/Unarmed) collapsed into Kinetic;
  the distinction is preserved by the separate "Wound Type" field.

HIDDEN COMBAT LEVEL (Phase 2): CL 1-100, never shown as a number.
CL anchor table in CombatData.CL_ANCHORS drives health, action,
damage, defense, armor, dodge, block, crit_resist.
Steamtek-native scale (CL1 = 50HP -> CL40 = 21000HP), NOT the
framework doc's literal numbers.

WEAPON FAMILIES (Phase 10) - Steamtek-native. The 12 existing

  Ranged keystone: Pistol, Assault Rifle, Sniper Rifle, Shotgun,
                   Grenade Launcher, Flame Thrower

PROFICIENCY TIERS (by points spent in the governing keystone):
  Untrained  0 pts    acc +0    speed +0%
  I          1-4      acc +10   speed +3%
  II         5-9      acc +25   speed +7%
  III        10-14    acc +45   speed +12%
  IV         15-18    acc +70   speed +18%

  SOURCE DECISION: proficiency is KEYSTONE-DERIVED (option a).
  Melee/Ranged points lift all six of their families equally, so
  families do not yet differentiate from each other. This is
  correct for a GENERALIST starting profession - per-family
  divergence is meant to arrive with the specialist professions
  (Sniper, Bombardier, etc.) as per-family nodes. Families are
  tracked INDIVIDUALLY in data so that upgrading to usage-based or
  hybrid later is a SOURCE SWAP, not a restructure.
  NOTE: usage-based leveling was considered and rejected - it is
  the UO/Elder Scrolls model, not SWG. SWG bought accuracy ranks
  deliberately in the tree. Usage-based also fights the gear-first
  pillar, punishes experimentation, and grinds badly in a roguelite.

XP ECONOMY (reworked 7-19)
-------------------------------------------------------------
THREE bugs were stacked here. All fixed.

1. XP WAS BEING DESTROYED. _add_skill_xp clamped every gain to
   _get_xp_type_cap(), which resolves to "cheapest unlocked keystone
   cost x 1.5" = 15. A kill awards 50-67, so most of every kill was
   silently thrown away. The cap was only ever meant to scale a
   DISPLAY BAR. Pools are now UNCAPPED; the function still exists
   for display scaling.
2. THE WHOLE TREE COST ~74 XP -- about two kills.
3. "cost" DID DOUBLE DUTY as both the XP price and the points
   consumed from the keystone budget.

POINTS AND XP ARE NOW SEPARATE CURRENCIES:
  "cost"     = POINTS consumed from the keystone's budget
  "xp_cost"  = the XP PRICE of the node

APPROVED PRICES:
  Keystone unlock     500 XP
  Ability node        300 XP
  Stat node           150 XP
  Crafting node        40 XP   <-- PLACEHOLDER, see below

Applied to 3 keystones, 6 ability nodes, 48 stat nodes, 16 crafting
nodes. Legacy path-profession xp_cost values were left untouched.

FULL STREET THUG BUILD = ~5,700 Combat XP
  ~114 Scrap Thief kills (50 XP) or ~85 Rust Marauders (67 XP).

The crafting node price of 40 is EXPLICITLY PROVISIONAL. It was
priced against sample/scan income that is going away (see RESOURCE
MODEL CHANGE). Re-price it once the scavenging loop exists and we
know what a recovery action pays. It is commented as a placeholder
in GameData.gd.

DISPLAY NOTE: the Street Thug diamond used to read "CXP x/y CFT x/y",
but those tallies always counted POINTS, not XP. With the split that

RESOURCE MODEL CHANGE (open design, blocks crafting phases)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Josh's read, and it matches the spec: THE CURRENT SAMPLING /
SURVEYING SYSTEM GOES AWAY.

Why it is incompatible: _generate_hotspot_set() spawns resource
hotspots AROUND THE PLAYER'S CURRENT POSITION at scan time. Spec 2.4
requires resources to be generated at campaign start from the
campaign seed, anchored to fixed locations, and permanent for that
campaign. Spec 5.4's example is "Aurelian Conductive Copper, Source:
Floor 12 Substation" -- a specific place, not wherever you stood.

REPLACEMENT DIRECTION: scavenging and recycling. Copper comes off a
scrap pile; batteries come out of a broken drone. The existing
DUMPSTER interaction (walk up, press E, receive Plastic) is already
structurally the right shape -- fixed location, defined yield,
repeatable. The new model is that pattern seeded across floors, with
materials carrying provenance (source_id, floor_id, location_id,
quality, primary trait, instability, extraction purity).

CONSEQUENCE FOR XP: Crafting XP income today is ONLY sample 5 /
scan 10 / craft 15. Two of those three disappear with sampling. That
is why crafting node prices are deferred.

OPEN QUESTION TO SETTLE EARLY: does SCAVENGING award Crafting XP, or
does XP come only from MAKING things? SWG paid for harvesting; the
spec's identity statement centres on materials -> experimentation ->
outcomes, which leans toward rewarding the craft. This decides
whether the loop is "grind nodes to level crafting" or "craft to
level crafting".

INCIDENT NOTES
-------------------------------------------------------------
1. PHASE 6b - BULK REPLACEMENT DELETED 725 LINES
A bulk text replacement used two DISTANT function names as block
boundaries and deleted ~725 lines / 30+ unrelated functions. Caught
by diffing the function list against the original; recovered from
the Phase 6a export with no loss. The redo used a boundary-aware
helper replacing ONE function at a time, verifying function count
after each step.
  LESSON: never delimit a replacement by a far-away anchor.
  Replace one function at a time and verify `grep -c '^func '`
  before and after.
A follow-up bug came from the same incident: the recovery rolled
back the `var enemy_nodes` declaration while the redo rebuilt only
the functions USING it, producing 9 "not declared in current scope"
errors.
  LESSON: when recovering from a snapshot, re-check that every
  declaration a refactor introduced still exists.

2. GAMEDATA DESYNC - 12 WEAPONS SILENTLY IGNORED ARMOR
Discovered 7-19. GameData.gd on BOTH machines was still the
pre-Phase-3 / pre-Phase-4b copy: old coefficients (1.05/1.25/1.5/0)
and old damage types (Slashing/Blunt/Unarmed/Explosive/Incendiary/
Kinetic). Phases 4c-7 only ever touched main/Combat/CombatData, so
the stale GameData survived several sessions unnoticed.
  IMPACT: CombatData builds each enemy's resistance dictionary
  keyed by the SEVEN new type names. apply_typed_mitigation does
  resistances.get(damage_type, 0) - so any weapon carrying an old
  type name matched nothing, returned rating 0, and took ZERO
  mitigation. 12 of 19 weapons bypassed enemy armor entirely, and
  the 7 "Kinetic" ones (actually guns) were mitigated as melee.
  The whole Phase 4c archetype-resistance system was inert in play.
  DETECTION: the lookup returns a legal 0 rather than erroring, so
  nothing ever complained.
  FIX: re-applied both approved transformations to GameData.gd and
  verified every weapon damage type has a matching resistance key.
  LESSON: a delivered file that is not installed on BOTH machines
  will eventually come back and overwrite the good copy. Install
  everywhere, or push to the repo, the same day.

3. QUALITY-50 CRAFTING DRAG (fixed 7-19)
Looted materials arrive as plain stacks with no stat block, but
_matches_requirement lets them satisfy recipe slots. They scored a
flat 50/1000 in _get_weighted_stack_score, silently dragging any
craft that used them into the bottom 5% of its stat range.
  FIX: statless materials no longer count toward crafted quality
  at all - quality comes from the sampled materials used. Neutral
  baseline when nothing carries stats is now 500, not 50.
  RELATED DESIGN CALL: looted materials were NOT made
  quality-bearing. The crafting spec sources all materials from
  campaign-seeded PERMANENT sources with provenance (source_id,
  floor_id, location_id, extraction_purity); enemy loot is not a
  material channel in that design. The spec's role for loot is
  BLUEPRINTS - "looting schematic data", recovering Steamtek
  artifacts, technical manuals.
  ALSO NOTED: 4 loot items (Damaged Circuit, Overcharged Coil,
  Rusted Gear, Scrap Metal Chunk) are used in NO recipe - dead
  weight. Natural fits as salvage/components or schematic carriers.

4. RIGHT_X ORPHAN (7-19)
While converting the KeystoneViewer right panel from fixed pixel
positions to anchors, the `var right_x` declaration was deleted but
two child controls still referenced it, producing "Identifier
right_x not declared in the current scope".
  LESSON: the mirror of incident 1. When a refactor REMOVES a
