STEAMTEK -- ENEMY SPAWN REFERENCE
=================================

Everything you can put in an ENEMY_SPAWN_TABLE entry, and what each
choice actually does. Generated from CombatData.gd.


HOW TO ADD AN ENEMY
-------------------
Add one block to ENEMY_SPAWN_TABLE in main.gd. That is the whole job --
the enemy is instantiated from enemy.tscn at startup and parented under
World/YSortLayer/Enemies. Nothing is hand-placed.

    {
        "display_name": "Blackline Enforcer",
        "cl": 12,                          # 1-100, hidden from the player
        "archetype": "Heavy",              # see ARCHETYPES below
        "faction": "Blackline Security",   # see FACTIONS below
        "position": Vector2(600, -200),    # world coords
        "sprite_scale": 1.0,
        "tint": Color(0.8, 0.85, 1.0),     # colours the sprite
        "kill_xp": 250,
        "loot_key": "Enemy2",              # which loot table to roll
        "attack_cooldown": 2.5,            # seconds between attacks
        "respawn_time": 30.0,
    },

Stats are DERIVED. You never type health or damage -- Combat Level sets
the tier, archetype and faction shape it from there.

ARCHETYPES -- what the enemy IS
-------------------------------
Archetype does two things: it shapes core stats, and it decides which
damage types get through. Pick it for the FIGHT you want.

Stat multipliers (applied to the CL base):

  Assault             1.00    1.05    1.00
  Brawler             1.15    1.00    0.90
  Commander           1.10    1.05    1.15
  Engineer            0.95    0.85    1.05
  Hacker              0.75    0.80    1.10
  Heavy               1.40    1.15    0.85

  Rifleman            0.90    1.10    1.00

Reading that table: a Heavy resists Kinetic at 1.5x armor but Arc at
only 0.6x, so arc weapons cut through it. A Hacker resists almost
nothing and folds to EMP (0.2x).


FACTIONS -- who they fight for
------------------------------
Faction is mostly IDENTITY. Every faction fields everything from pawns
to queens -- that spread comes from COMBAT LEVEL, not from faction.
The multipliers below only express gear quality at IDENTICAL CL.

  Blackline Security
    Corporate military contractors.
    health x1.05   damage x1.10   armor x1.15

  Ghost Circuit
    Cybernetically enhanced mercenaries.
    health x0.95   damage x1.15   armor x0.90

  Reactor Authority
    Industrial government security.
    health x1.15   damage x1.00   armor x1.20

  Rust Syndicate
    Street gangs and scavengers.
    health x0.90   damage x0.95   armor x0.85


DAMAGE TYPES
------------
  Kinetic, Ballistic, Thermal, Pressure, Arc, Chemical, EMP

  Chemical and EMP have NO weapons yet, so those resistance columns do
  nothing in play. Weapons currently deal Kinetic, Ballistic, Thermal,
  Pressure or Arc.

PICKING A GOOD TEST ENEMY
-------------------------
- Want to SEE typed resistances? Pick an archetype with a wide spread
  (Heavy, Engineer, Hacker) and attack it with two different damage
  types. The difference should be obvious.
- Want to see dodge/block fire? They scale with CL. Below about CL10
  they almost never trigger.
- Want to see rank marks differ? Anything CL6+ reads differently from
  your CL1/CL5 starters.
- Player has 500 HP. A CL20 Heavy hits for ~300 and will kill you in
  two swings. CL12 is around 138 per hit -- dangerous but survivable.
